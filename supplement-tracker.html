You're Spending Money on Supplements You Might Not Need

Americans spend approximately $60 billion annually on dietary supplements—that's roughly $200 per person. Yet despite this massive investment in our health, most supplement users are flying blind. You take a vitamin, an herb, a nootropic, and hope for the best.

The problem is that you can't feel the difference. Does your magnesium actually help you sleep better, or are you sleeping better because you've had less stress this week? Is your energy high because of the creatine, or because you've been exercising more? The placebo effect is real—research suggests 30-40% of the benefit people experience from supplements may be placebo rather than pharmacological.

Without tracking, you're essentially guessing. You might take the same 5 supplements for a year, spending $300, when only 2 of them actually work for you. Or worse, you might quit something that's genuinely helping because you didn't have concrete evidence it was working.

The solution is data. Track what you take alongside how you feel. After 3-4 weeks, patterns emerge. You discover which supplements correlate with better sleep, more stable energy, clearer thinking, or fewer symptoms. You stop wasting money on supplements that don't work for you, and you double down on the ones that do.

How It Works in 3 Steps

Transform supplement guessing into evidence-based decisions.

1

Log What You Take

Each morning, record which supplements you took and at what dose. It takes 30 seconds. Add the supplement from ReactLog's built-in database or create custom entries. Track dose, timing, and even batch/brand if you want to compare.

2

Rate How You Feel

Throughout the day, check in with how you feel—mood, energy, focus, sleep quality, and any symptoms you're tracking. The more often you log, the more data ReactLog has to work with. Even a quick 10-second mood check 2-3 times a day dramatically improves your insights. Quick sliders, not tedious forms.

3

See the Correlations

After 2-3 weeks of consistent logging, ReactLog's on-device analytics surface patterns: "Your energy is 23% higher on days you take creatine + sleep 7+ hours." The more mood and symptom data you've logged, the more confident these correlations become. You see exactly which supplements correlate with your best days.

Your Supplement Data Stays on Your Phone

Why privacy matters for supplement tracking.

Your supplement use reveals things about you that are deeply personal: health conditions you're managing, mental health concerns, lifestyle choices, vulnerabilities. You don't want that data on someone else's server.

Traditional apps sell your health data to data brokers, insurance companies, and advertisers. Some are sold even when companies claim they won't be. ReactLog takes a fundamentally different approach:

  • No backend servers — Your data lives on your device only. Period.
  • No analytics SDKs — We don't send telemetry about your usage patterns.
  • No data collection — We have nothing to collect. No servers means no data pipeline.
  • No account required — You don't need to create a login. No username, no email, nothing that identifies you.
  • Face ID/Touch ID lock — Your supplement data is biometrically protected on your phone.

We charge for the app instead of charging you with your privacy. It's a sustainable business model that aligns our interests with yours. We want you to use ReactLog, but we don't want your data.
