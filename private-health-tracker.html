Your Health App Is Probably Selling Your Data

Health apps routinely share data with advertisers, insurers, and data brokers. In 2024, the FTC fined multiple health apps for sharing sensitive health data without explicit user consent. Period tracking apps were scrutinized following the Dobbs decision for potential data subpoenas in jurisdictions with abortion restrictions. Mental health apps have been caught sharing therapy session data with third-party analytics providers. Even apps that claim "we don't sell your data" often use analytics SDKs that send detailed usage data to third parties.

Here's what gets collected and shared:

What you track

Every symptom, mood shift, medication, and entry reveals health conditions

When you track

Frequency and timing patterns expose behavior and health urgency

Your patterns

Mood cycles, medication routines, symptom correlations reveal diagnoses

The business model is clear: if you're not paying for the app, you're the product. Health apps aren't unique — this is how most free software works. But health data is different. Your health tracking data reveals mental health conditions, reproductive choices, substance use, chronic illness status, and medication history. This isn't just personal. It's weaponizable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does ReactLog really not collect any data?
Correct. There are no backend servers. No analytics SDKs of any kind. No cloud infrastructure. Your data exists only on your iPhone. We cannot collect data we have no access to. Every query, calculation, and analysis happens on your device.
How does ReactLog make money if they don't sell data?
ReactLog is a paid subscription app with a 14-day free trial. The business model is simple: build a product good enough that people choose to pay for it. We don't need to monetize user data because we're funded by users who value the product. No ads, no data sales, no venture capitalists demanding exponential growth through data harvesting.
Can my health data be subpoenaed?
Since your data exists only on your personal device, it cannot be obtained from ReactLog — there's literally nothing on our servers to hand over. This is the architectural advantage of having no servers. Law enforcement cannot subpoena data we don't possess.
Is ReactLog HIPAA compliant?
ReactLog doesn't store, transmit, or process health data on any server, so HIPAA's server-side requirements don't apply in the traditional sense. Your data stays entirely on your device, under your control. If you're a healthcare provider subject to HIPAA, consult your legal team, but the architecture is fundamentally private by design.
What if I lose my phone?
Your data is stored locally, so if you lose your device without a backup, the data would be lost. This is the tradeoff of true privacy — complete on-device control means local storage risk. We recommend regular iPhone backups via iCloud or Finder. Your backups are encrypted by iOS.
